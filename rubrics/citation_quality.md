# Rubric: Citation Quality

## What this rubric checks
Whether the citations provided are relevant to the answer and not spurious.

**Important:** You do NOT have access to the page content at these URLs. Assess
relevance solely from the URL path and slug (e.g. a slug containing the key topic
term is clearly relevant; a slug about an entirely different domain is not). Do not
fail a citation just because you cannot verify its content — that is not your job here.

## Pass criteria
- All cited URLs are relevant to the topic of the question.
- Citations are not from completely unrelated corpus pages (e.g., citing a cooking page
  for an engineering question).
- At least one citation is clearly relevant to the answer content.

## Fail criteria
- Citations are entirely irrelevant to the answer topic (e.g. a recipe URL cited for
  a space question, or a knot-tying URL for a robotics question).
- All citations are clearly noise pages unrelated to the question topic.
- No citations are provided for a factual answer.
